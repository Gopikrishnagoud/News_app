General questions of physics

Question Answer
What is the SI unit of force? Newton (N)
What is the formula for acceleration? Acceleration (a) = Change in velocity (Δv) / Time taken (Δt)
What is the relationship between force, mass, and acceleration? Newton's Second Law: F = ma (Force = Mass × Acceleration)
What is the SI unit of energy? Joule (J)
What is the law of conservation of energy? Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.
What is the formula for kinetic energy? Kinetic Energy (KE) = 0.5 × Mass × (Velocity)2
What is the formula for potential energy? Potential Energy (PE) = Mass × Gravity × Height
What is the law of universal gravitation? Every particle attracts every other particle in the universe with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers.
What is the acceleration due to gravity on Earth? Approximately 9.81 m/s2
What is the SI unit of power? Watt (W)
What is the formula for power? Power (P) = Work (W) / Time (t)
What is the formula for work? Work (W) = Force (F) × Distance (d) × cos(θ) (where θ is the angle between the force and the direction of motion)
What is Newton's First Law of Motion? An object at rest will stay at rest, and an object in motion will stay in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.
What is Newton's Second Law of Motion? The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass (F = ma).
What is Newton's Third Law of Motion? For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
What is momentum? Momentum is the product of an object's mass and its velocity. It is a vector quantity.
What is the formula for momentum? Momentum (p) = Mass (m) × Velocity (v)
What is impulse? Impulse is the change in momentum of an object when a force is applied over a period of time.
What is the formula for impulse? Impulse (J) = Force (F) × Time (Δt)
What is friction? Friction is the force that opposes the motion of one surface over another.
What factors affect the force of friction? The force of friction depends on the nature of the surfaces in contact, the force pressing them together (normal force), and sometimes the relative motion between the surfaces.
What is static friction? Static friction is the frictional force that prevents two surfaces from sliding past each other when no external force is applied.
What is kinetic friction? Kinetic friction is the frictional force between two surfaces in motion relative to each other.
What is centripetal force? Centripetal force is the force that keeps an object moving in a circular path, directed towards the center of the circle.
What is the formula for centripetal force? Centripetal Force (Fc) = (Mass (m) × (Velocity (v))2) / Radius (r)
What is projectile motion? Projectile motion is the motion of an object thrown or projected into the air, subject only to the force of gravity and air resistance (if present).
What is the trajectory of a projectile? The trajectory of a projectile is the curved path it follows under the influence of gravity.
What is the formula for the range of a projectile? Range = (Initial Velocity)2 × sin(2θ) / g (where θ is the angle of projection and g is the acceleration due to gravity)
What is the formula for the period of a pendulum? Period (T) = 2π√(Length (L) / g) (where g is the acceleration due to gravity)
What is the law of reflection? The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection, and both lie in the same plane as the incident ray and the normal.
What is the law of refraction? The ratio of the sine of the angle of incidence to the sine of the angle of refraction is a constant, known as the refractive index.
What is the Doppler effect? The change in frequency or wavelength of a wave in relation to an observer who is moving relative to the wave source.
What is the principle of superposition? When two or more waves overlap, the resultant displacement at any point is equal to the vector sum of the displacements of the individual waves at that point.
What is resonance? Resonance occurs when the frequency of an external force matches the natural frequency of an object, resulting in a dramatic increase in amplitude.
What is the formula for wavelength? Wavelength (λ) = Speed of light (c) / Frequency (f)
What is the formula for frequency? Frequency (f) = Speed of light (c) / Wavelength (λ)
What is Ohm's Law? Ohm's Law states that the current passing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points, provided the temperature remains constant.
What is the formula for Ohm's Law? Voltage (V) = Current (I) × Resistance (R)
What is electrical resistance? Electrical resistance is the opposition to the flow of electric current in a conductor. It is measured in ohms.
What is electric current? Electric current is the flow of electric charge. It is measured in amperes (A).
What is voltage? Voltage is the electric potential difference between two points. It is measured in volts (V).
What is capacitance? Capacitance is the ability of a system to store an electric charge. It is measured in farads (F).
What is inductance? Inductance is the property of an electrical conductor by which a change in current flowing through it induces an electromotive force (emf) in both the conductor itself and in any nearby conductors.
What is the SI unit of electric charge? Coulomb (C)
What is the formula for electric charge? Electric Charge (Q) = Current (I) × Time (t)
What is the formula for electric power? Electric Power (P) = Voltage (V) × Current (I)
What is the SI unit of electric power? Watt (W)
